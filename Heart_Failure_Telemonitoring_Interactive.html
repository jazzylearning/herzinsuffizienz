Patient Selection Criteria

Inclusion Criteria

  • NYHA Class II-III symptoms
  • HF hospitalization within 12 months
  • LVEF ≤40% (HFrEF) - Class I evidence
  • LVEF >40% (HFpEF) - Class IIa evidence
  • Elevated NT-proBNP
  • Age ≥18 years
Sources: ESC Guidelines 2021 [6], TIM-HF2 [3], CHAMPION [5], GUIDE-HF [7]

Exclusion Criteria

  • End-stage HF / palliative phase
  • Life expectancy <6 months
  • Cognitive impairment preventing use
  • Major depression (PHQ-9 ≥10)
  • Terminal renal insufficiency
  • Recent/planned cardiac procedures
Sources: TIM-HF2 [3], Netherlands Protocol [9], HerzMobil [11]

Organizational Structure

Four-Level Care Model (Netherlands Protocol)

Level 1
Technical Support
  • • Patient onboarding
  • • App installation
  • • Technical troubleshooting
  • • Missed measurement follow-up
Level 2
Monitoring Nurses
  • • Interpret alarms
  • • Contact patients
  • • Facilitate medication changes
  • • Weekly supervision meetings
Level 3
HF Specialists
  • • Supervise Level 2
  • • Titrate GDMT
  • • Individualize thresholds
  • • Protocol transitions
Level 4
Cardiologists
  • • Complex case management
  • • Complication management
  • • Final clinical authority
  • • Advanced interventions
Source: Jansen et al., Netherlands Nationwide Protocol, EHJ Digital Health 2024 [9]

Unstable Protocol (Intensive Phase)

Enrollment Triggers:
  • Hospital discharge (congested HF)
  • Initial HF clinic visit
  • Ambulatory exacerbation
Monitoring Intensity:
  • Vitals: Daily Monday-Friday
  • Symptoms: Weekly questionnaire
  • Alert review: Daily by Level 2-3
  • Medication titration: Every 7-14 days
Transition to Stable:
OMT achieved + stable 2-4 weeks + euvolemia
Sources: Netherlands Protocol [9], HerzMobil Tirol [4]

Stable Protocol (Maintenance Phase)

Enrollment Criteria:
  • Optimal medical therapy achieved
  • Clinical stability demonstrated
  • No recent diuretic adjustments
Monitoring Intensity:
  • Vitals: At least weekly
  • Symptoms: Weekly questionnaire
  • Alert review: As triggered
  • Face-to-face: Every 1-3 months
Re-escalation Triggers:
Symptom worsening, weight gain, re-admission
Sources: Netherlands Protocol [9], HerzMobil Tirol [4]

Strategic Recommendations

01

Implement Risk-Stratified Patient Selection

Prioritize NYHA Class II-III with recent hospitalization. Include both HFrEF and HFpEF patients.

ESC Guidelines [6] TIM-HF2 [3] CHAMPION [5]
02

Deploy Tiered Monitoring Technology

Match monitoring intensity to risk: Invasive PAP for high-risk NYHA III, daily non-invasive for moderate, weekly for stable.

CHAMPION [5] TIM-HF2 [3] Meta-analysis [2]
03

Establish Centralized Telemedical Center

Create 24/7 staffed center with tiered expertise (Level 1-4). Implement doc-to-doc communication with primary care.

TIM-HF2 [3] Netherlands [9] HerzMobil [4]
04

Standardize Alert Thresholds with Individualization

Use evidence-based defaults with physician override capability. Prevent alert fatigue while maintaining safety.

Brons et al. [12] Netherlands [9] TIM-HF2 [3]
05

Implement Two-Phase Care Protocols

Unstable (daily M-F) vs Stable (weekly) protocols with clear transition criteria and re-escalation pathways.

Netherlands [9] HerzMobil [4]
